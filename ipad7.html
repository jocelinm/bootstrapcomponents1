iPad 7

Source: Swappa https://swappa.com/guide/apple-ipad-7th-gen/specs

  • The iPad 10.2-inch (officially the iPad 7th generation) is an Apple Inc. tablet computer. It has a 10.2-inch Retina display and is powered by an A10 Fusion processor from Apple. It is the replacement for the 9.7-inch 6th generation iPad. The device was unveiled on September 10, 2019, and was made available on September 25, 2019. The dimensions of the seventh generation iPad are as follows: 9.8" (H) x 6.8" (W) x 0.29" (D). The display size on this model is 10.2 inches. The WiFi-only model is 1.07 pounds, while the WiFi + Cellular model is 1.09 pounds. The iPad (7th generation) has a 10.2-inch multi-touch Retina display with LED backlighting and IPS technology. The display has a 2160 x 1620 resolution, 264 PPI, SDR brightness of 500 nits, and a fingerprint-resistant oleophobic coating. The iPad (7th generation) has a front-facing 1.2MP FaceTime HD camera and a rear-facing 8MP wide-angle camera. The front-facing camera has an f/2.4 aperture, HDR for photos, a Retina Flash, cinematic video stabilization, and 720p HD video recording. Some of these features are shared by the rear camera, such as an f/2.4 aperture and HDR for photos, but it can record video at 1080p HD at 30 frames per second. The iPad 7th generation is powered by a 32.4Wh battery and charges via the Lighting connector. The iPad's battery life is expected to last up to 10 hours when surfing the web via WiFi, or up to 9 hours when surfing the web via cellular connection.
